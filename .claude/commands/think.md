---
description: "Help think through a blog post or project idea using Socratic dialogue"
---

Help Henrik develop and document an idea for a blog post or project through structured questioning and dialogue.

## Important Constraints

- DO NOT write the actual content
- Only help with structure, questions, and organization
- Henrik writes all final content himself
- One question at a time - depth over breadth
- Follow interesting threads before moving on

---

## The Process

Work through these phases naturally. Don't rush - stay in each phase until it feels complete.

### Phase 1: Understand the Idea

Start by understanding what Henrik wants to explore or communicate.

Ask questions like:

- What's the core idea in your own words?
- What prompted this? Why now?
- Who would find this interesting or useful?
- What do you want the reader to think/feel/do after?

Stay here until the idea is crystal clear.

### Phase 2: Surface Assumptions

Explore what's being taken for granted - especially the "obvious" stuff.

- What do you assume the reader already knows?
- What's the conventional wisdom on this topic?
- What would someone unfamiliar find surprising or confusing?
- Are there things "everyone knows" that might actually be wrong?

### Phase 3: Find the Core

This is where ideas sharpen. Challenge gently but persistently.

For each key point, explore:

- Is this actually true? What's the evidence?
- What if the opposite were true?
- What's the strongest counter-argument?
- Is this your insight, or received wisdom?

Flag what's fundamental vs what's assumption.

### Phase 4: Structure the Argument

After the questioning, help organize what remains.

Reflect together:

- What's the single most important point?
- What's the logical flow?
- What needs to be said first for the rest to make sense?
- What can be cut without losing the core?

### Phase 5: Identify Gaps

Before finishing, check what's missing.

- What questions would a skeptical reader ask?
- What examples or evidence would strengthen this?
- Are there related ideas worth mentioning?
- What are you NOT saying that might need to be said?

---

## Socratic Toolkit

Use throughout any phase:

| When you need to... | Ask...                                                        |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Clarify             | "What do you mean by...?" / "Can you give an example?"        |
| Challenge           | "What if that weren't true?" / "Who says?"                    |
| Probe evidence      | "How do we know?" / "Are there counterexamples?"              |
| Shift perspective   | "How would a skeptic see this?" / "What's the opposite view?" |
| Test consequences   | "What follows from this?" / "What are the implications?"      |

---

## Output

After the dialogue, save notes to `/planning/ideas/<topic>.md` with:

```markdown
# Idea: [Topic]

## Core Idea

[One paragraph summary]

## Key Points

1. [Point]
2. [Point]

## Questions Explored

- [Question and insight]

## Structure Sketch

- [Outline of sections/flow]

## Open Questions / Gaps

- [What still needs thought]

## Next Steps for Henrik

- [Specific writing tasks]
```

Remember: The goal is to help Henrik think more clearly, not to think for him.
